The solar system is a gravitationally bound system of the Sun and the objects that orbit it. It formed 4.6 billion years ago from the gravitational collapse of a giant interstellar molecular cloud.

The four inner system planets are Mercury, Venus, Earth and Mars, which are terrestrial planets.

The four outer system planets are Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus and Neptune. Jupiter and Saturn are gas giants. The two outermost planets, Uranus and Neptune, are ice giants.
